Confidential Client Engagement

Designed and built a confidential investor intelligence CRM under a non-disclosure agreement for a private client. The platform centralizes investor research, relationship management, due diligence, outreach tracking, and fundraising workflows into a single operating environment.

A core component of the system is a curated database of 500+ OSINT-verified seed investor profiles, assembled through structured open-source intelligence research and multi-source verification. Each profile is intended to provide defensible, evidence-backed information to support capital formation efforts while maintaining source provenance and auditability.

Findings

The research effort demonstrated that publicly circulated investor lists often contain outdated, duplicated, or poorly substantiated information. Applying systematic OSINT verification significantly reduced noise while increasing confidence in the remaining records.

Key findings included:

  • Built a database of 500+ OSINT-verified seed investor profiles supported by documented public sources.
  • Eliminated duplicate, stale, and unverifiable records through structured verification.
  • Identified consistent data-quality issues across widely shared fundraising databases.
  • Established a repeatable verification methodology emphasizing provenance, traceability, and confidence scoring.
  • Organized investor information to support due diligence, outreach prioritization, relationship tracking, and fundraising workflows.
  • Demonstrated that smaller, evidence-backed datasets often provide greater operational value than substantially larger, unverified collections.

Sidebar

Designed and developed Sidebar, a confidential legal intelligence and case management platform created for law firms to organize complex matters, consolidate evidence, and accelerate factual analysis.

Sidebar combines case management with structured legal intelligence workflows by integrating document analysis, evidence organization, timeline reconstruction, and OSINT-assisted research into a unified workspace. The platform supports the development of client, witness, and entity profiles using lawfully obtained public information, while applying a structured fact-analysis methodology that transforms unstructured information into organized, traceable knowledge suitable for legal review.

The system emphasizes source provenance, auditability, and explainable AI to assist attorneys in organizing information—not replacing legal judgment.

Findings

Development of Sidebar produced several operational findings regarding legal information management and investigative workflows.

  • Demonstrated that structured conceptualization substantially improves the organization of complex legal matters by transforming scattered documents into interconnected people, events, locations, organizations, and evidence.
  • Applied fact particularization to decompose broad narratives into discrete, verifiable factual assertions, making inconsistencies, evidentiary gaps, and corroborating information easier to identify.
  • Used OSINT-derived public information, where appropriate and legally obtained, to enrich client, witness, and entity profiles while preserving source attribution and traceability.
  • Reduced information fragmentation by linking documents, timelines, evidence, notes, and entities within a unified matter workspace.
  • Established an evidence-centered workflow emphasizing provenance, confidence assessment, and auditability for every significant factual assertion.
  • Demonstrated that AI-assisted retrieval and structured organization can substantially reduce the time required to review large case files while maintaining attorney oversight and decision-making authority.
  • Reinforced the importance of explainable outputs, where every generated insight can be traced back to supporting documents or publicly available sources.

02 — Method

How I work.

Five operating rules. Each one was earned in production — on specific builds and specific failures.

M·01

Blank means unverified

Every entry needs a primary source. Without one, the field stays blank — so the record shows exactly what is known and what isn't.

M·02

Break your own build first

Every system gets an adversarial audit by its author before anyone else sees it. The bug you find in private is the reputation you keep in public.

M·03

Tamper-evident by design

Every significant change is logged, and any edit leaves evidence — including the operator's. Accountability is built into the system, not added as policy.

M·04

Confidence is a number

Recommendations ship with an explicit score, named assumptions, and what would change the answer. A seven out of ten with its unknowns listed beats a confident story.

M·05

Govern before you automate

Capability without governance is faster liability. Every system needs a defensible reason, an owner, and a trail before the workflow ships.
